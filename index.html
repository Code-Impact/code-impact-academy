Start here

What this is (and what it honestly isn't)

This is not an exam course. It's a guided programme where you build small things every week and pick up the words, tools and habits that real software, cloud and DevOps teams use.

🫱

Honest expectation. Twelve weeks won't make you a senior engineer — nobody's twelve weeks do. What it will give you: a solid foundation, a small portfolio you're proud of, and the confidence to keep learning like a junior engineer. That's the whole goal.

Who it's for

🎓

Fresh starters

Graduates or career changers with little or no professional software experience. No cloud, DevOps or AI background needed.

🔁

People who practise

You're curious and happy to try, get stuck, and try again. That habit matters more than any talent.

⏱️

Around 8–10 hrs/week

Enough time to watch, build and demo each week. Comfortable learning in English.

The rhythm

How each week works

Same four beats every week, so you always know what to do next.

STEP 01
📺

Watch & try

2–3 short clips, then repeat one small guided exercise yourself.

STEP 02
🔨

Build

Make one small project, or add one real feature to your main app.

STEP 03
✍️

Write

A one-page note: what I learned, what was hard, what I tried.

STEP 04
🎤

Show

A 5-minute Friday demo. Show it, explain it simply, take one question.

The one rule

Don't copy and paste code you can't explain. AI can help you learn — but you must be able to say what your code actually does.

💡

Choosing videos: prefer recent clips for fast-changing tools (cloud consoles, AI APIs); older is fine for Git or HTML. Use official docs only when you need an exact command or setup step.

Get set up

Before Week 1

The best first lesson isn't a lecture. You'll see a tiny website work, then make your own. Get these ready first.

✅ Your checklist

  • ✓ A laptop (Windows or Mac), internet and a modern browser.
  • ✓ A personal GitHub account and a Google account.
  • ✓ VS Code installed, Git installed, and a working terminal.
  • ✓ A profile photo or avatar (optional) for your Week 1 page.
  • ✓ A notebook — paper or digital — for your weekly note.

The tone from day one

It's fine not to know. It's not fine to stay silent, copy blindly, or give up at the first error. Engineers learn by asking, testing and trying again.
